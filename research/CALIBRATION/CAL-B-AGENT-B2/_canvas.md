## SECTION 1: Niche Identity & Strategic Rationale

### 1.1 Niche Name & Definition

- **Niche name:** B2B Fractional Executive Services for Mid-Market Companies `[P]`
- **One-line definition:** Companies providing ongoing, part-time C-suite executives (CFO, CTO, CMO, COO) to mid-market companies (50-500 employees, EUR 5M-100M revenue) on subscription or retainer — embedded leadership, not project-based consulting. `[P]`
- **Why this niche exists now:** Three converging forces: (1) the structural shift from full-time to fractional work (25-40% of US mid-market companies now use fractional executives, projected to hit 50%+ by 2027), (2) the "mid-market leadership gap" — companies too large for founder-operating and too small for full C-suite budgets, and (3) Gartner projection that 30%+ of midsize enterprises will have at least one fractional executive on retainer by 2027. The model has passed from "experiment" to "default option." `[E]` — multiple independent sources (Vendux, Gartner, JRG Partners, Dataintelo)
- **Ansoff matrix position:** Market Development — existing service category (executive leadership) applied to a consolidating niche (mid-market, not startups or enterprises). ClarityRev would offer a NEW service in this category. `[P]`

### 1.2 MECE Boundaries

**IN:**
- Companies with 50-500 employees or EUR 5M-100M revenue
- Using fractional services as ONGOING retainer/subscription (not project-based)
- B2B companies (product or service businesses, B2B SaaS, professional services, manufacturing, distribution)
- Buyer: CEO/Founder, CRO, or Board (economic decision-maker)
- Geography: NL/Benelux primary, EU secondary (DACH, UK, Nordics)
- Engagement model: Retainer-based, 1-3 days/week, 6-18 month typical duration

**OUT:**
- Startups (<50 employees, <EUR 5M revenue) — different buyer psychology, VC-funded dynamics, smaller deals
- Large enterprises (>500 employees, >EUR 100M revenue) — procurement processes, different competitor set
- Pure consulting firms (project-based advice, no embedded leadership) — McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte
- Interim management (full-time temporary, not part-time ongoing) — different engagement model
- Solo fractional practitioners (individuals, not agencies/platforms) — different business model, harder to productize
- Executive search firms (placement, not delivery) — Robert Half, Korn Ferry
- Staffing agencies (temps, not executives) — Randstad, Adecco
- C2C marketplaces (freelancer-direct) — Fiverr, Upwork

**Boundary-testing edge cases:**
1. A 45-employee SaaS company at EUR 4M revenue using a fractional CFO 2 days/week — OUT (below employee threshold, startup-stage). Rationale: different buying dynamics, VC-fundable, smaller deal size.
2. A PE-backed manufacturing company (200 employees, EUR 80M revenue) using a fractional COO for portfolio ops — IN. Rationale: classic mid-market profile, retainer engagement, PE portfolio company.
3. A 600-employee logistics company with a fractional CMO at 1 day/week — OUT (above employee threshold). Rationale: likely has full-time executives, fractional is advisory-only, lower urgency.

### 1.3 Niche Economics

- **Business model:** RET-based — fractional executive agencies/platforms charge retainer fees (monthly) to client companies. Commission model also exists (20-30% of executive's rate for marketplace platforms). `[P]` — observable.
- **Typical revenue per customer/transaction:** EUR 4,000-12,000/month per fractional executive role. Most common: EUR 6,000-8,000/month for 2 days/week at senior level. `[E]` — supported by Inside Partners, Fractional-CSuite, and multiple 2026 pricing guides.
- **Typical gross margin:** Platform/agency margins range 10-25% (revenue share with fractional executives). Agency-led models have higher margins (30-40%) but higher costs. Productized/tech-enabled models could achieve 60-80% margins at scale (ClarityRev's AI-delivered model). `[H]` — inferred from analog marketplaces and consulting margin benchmarks.
- **Key unit economics:** Estimated CAC: EUR 2,000-8,000 per client (warm referral: EUR 1,000-3,000; cold outbound: EUR 5,000-15,000). LTV: EUR 48,000-144,000 at EUR 4,000-8,000/mo × 12-18 month typical engagement. LTV:CAC ratio: 6:1 to 24:1 depending on channel. `[H]` — inferred from general B2B SaaS benchmarks (HubSpot Sales Benchmarks 2024, Pacific Crest SaaS Survey).
- **This matters because:** Revenue leakage in this niche means lost retainer revenue (client churn before 12 months) AND lost expansion revenue (failing to upsell from 1 role to 2-3 roles per client). The economics reward long-tenured multi-role clients. `[P]` — logical deduction.

### 1.9 Niche Risk Assessment

**Pre-mortem:** "It's 18 months from now. ClarityRev failed in the fractional executive niche. Why? The onboarding was deceptively simple — agencies loved the Snapshot demo, signed up, but never integrated it into their workflow. They saw the signals but didn't act on them because their operational rhythm was built around spreadsheets and phone calls, not CRM tasks. The free Snapshot produced impressive numbers, but churn hit 40% by month 6 because the recurring service felt like 'another dashboard we don't check.' We couldn't build the habit loop. Meanwhile, GoFractional and Catalant launched their own signal-detection features as free add-ons. Our wedge — the Snapshot — was replicated. We had no distribution moat because we never locked up the top 5 agencies; we went broad instead of deep. Three agencies produced 80% of our revenue, and one was acquired by a competitor. The niche thesis depended on 'agencies act on signals' — they didn't. We should have focused on 5 agencies, embedded in their weekly operations, and built the habit loop before scaling." `[P]` — judgment.

**Wrong-niche indicators (5 specific, measurable):**
1. Zero Snapshot-to-paid conversion after 5 free Snapshots for agencies (each represents 20+ Bob hours) — "agency likes the demo but never implements"
2. Every agency says "we already track this in our CRM/notion/spreadsheet" — the current solution is already good enough
3. Average engagement duration <4 months — the recurring service doesn't stick
4. Churn rate >8%/month by month 6 — unsustainable unit economics
5. No agency refers another agency within 90 days — the product has no viral loop in this niche

**Survivorship bias check:**
- Structural attractiveness (from §1.5 Five Forces): 2.4/5 — structurally challenging. `[H]`.
- Distribution advantage: LOW — ClarityRev has no existing fractional agency relationships. Wesley's network has some relevant contacts but no warm relationship with a fractional executive agency. `[P]`.
- **Recommendation driver:** This is a calibration niche. The recommendation is driven by the niche's intrinsic merits as a test case for data-sparse evaluation, not by a warm prospect. `[P]`.

**Single-point-of-failure assumption:** "Fractional agencies will adopt a signal-detection/revenue-intelligence tool." Stress test: If the adoption rate is 50% lower than estimated (10% trial-to-paid instead of 20%), does the thesis hold? At EUR 2,000/mo per agency, 5 agencies = EUR 120K/yr ARR. At 100 outreach attempts (40-80 hrs), CAC = EUR 8K-16K, payback = 4-8 months. Survives but unattractive. If churn >5%/mo, payback extends beyond 12 months — thesis breaks. Minimum viable adoption: ≥15% trial-to-paid + <5% monthly churn to reach EUR 120K ARR within 12 months. `[H]`.

**Scenario planning — 3 alternative futures:**
- **Optimistic:** Fractional executive market continues 15%+ growth. ClarityRev locks up the top 10 NL agencies as signal-intelligence partners. Expansion revenue from multi-role monitoring per client doubles ARPU. Agency-based distribution becomes moat.
- **Pessimistic:** GoFractional, Catalant, or another platform embeds signal detection into their core platform as a free feature. ClarityRev's differentiation collapses. Agencies see us as a "nice-to-have" and churn at 10%/mo.
- **Disruptive:** AI makes signal detection commoditized (many vendors offer it). The real value shifts to RECOVERY — automated execution on signals. ClarityRev's competitive moat depends on building the recovery workflow, not just the detection pipeline.

**Recommendation bet:** Versatile (leaning pessimistic). Hedge: build for HubSpot-native agencies first (low integration cost), validate adoption with 3 agencies before scaling.
